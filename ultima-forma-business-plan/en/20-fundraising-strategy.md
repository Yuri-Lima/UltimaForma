# Fundraising Strategy

## Infrastructure Investor Thesis

Ultima Forma is an infrastructure play, not an application. The company is building the identity orchestration layer -- analogous to what Stripe built for payments, Plaid for financial data, Twilio for communications, and Cloudflare for web infrastructure.

Infrastructure investments share core characteristics that make them attractive to specialized funds:

- **Winner-take-most dynamics.** Network effects in infrastructure create defensible market positions. The first platform to assemble critical mass in a market segment becomes the default.
- **Compounding moats.** Each new participant (issuer, verifier, developer, wallet user) increases the value of the network for all others. The moat grows with the ecosystem, not against it.
- **Open protocol leverage.** The open protocol creates an adoption surface that proprietary competitors cannot match. Developer adoption, regulatory acceptance, and third-party ecosystem growth all accelerate because the trust layer is open.
- **High gross margins at scale.** Infrastructure gross margins (85%+) compound with volume as fixed costs amortize across growing transaction base.
- **Long customer lifetimes.** Enterprise infrastructure is deeply integrated and difficult to replace, producing high LTV and low churn.

**Comparable infrastructure outcomes:** Stripe (US$ 65B+ valuation), Plaid (US$ 13B+ valuation), Twilio (US$ 15B+ market cap at peak), Cloudflare (US$ 30B+ market cap). These companies share the same core pattern: neutral infrastructure, open developer tools, proprietary network, compounding network effects.

## Objectives per Round

### Pre-seed

**Target:** **BRL 3.5 million**

**Objective:** Deliver production MVP, close paid pilots, and structure the commercial base for recurrence (subscription and first annual contracts). Publish open-source verification libraries and wallet SDK.

**Target runway*:** **16--18 months** (assuming average burn of ~**BRL 220k/month**, with hiring ramp)

### Seed

**Target:** **BRL 12 million**

**Objective:** Scale acquisition and retention, consolidate annual contracts, and prepare the company for Series A with efficiency metrics (LTV:CAC, payback) and revenue predictability. Grow developer community and ecosystem partner base.

**Target runway*:** **18--24 months** (average burn **BRL 480--550k/month**, with commercial and engineering expansion)

## Runway* Assumptions

- **Estimated monthly burn rate (average): BRL 220k/month**
  - Typical ramp: **BRL 180k/month** (months 1--3) → **BRL 250k/month** (months 10--18)
  - Compatible with the fixed base of cloud/observability/security and partial operation described in the "Unit Economics" section, with growth per clients and SLAs
- **Hired in pre-seed (core team): 6 FTE** (with 1--2 optional positions by traction)
- **Revenue (conservative, but not "zero"):** first paying clients from **M6--M9**, with focus on **Starter/Growth subscription** and pilots at early adopter price. Design partners (non-revenue) may precede paying clients by 3--6 months.
- **Gross margin:** high from the start (low variable COGS), but **burn** is dominated by team, reliability, and acquisition/sales (go-to-market) (per "Unit Economics" section)

## Expected Milestones (Pre-seed → Seed)

- **Product**: MVP in production with audit trails and **basic** and **qualified** verification stabilized, plus minimum operation automation (alerts, rate limiting, evidence per event). Open-source verification library and wallet SDK published on GitHub.
- **Traction**: **3--6 paying clients** (mix of pilots + at least **1--2** subscriptions), with at least **1 replicable use case** (playbook).
- **Revenue**: start of recurrence, with target of **MRR* BRL 40--80k** at end of period (conservative), prioritizing base quality and retention.
- **Efficiency**: first signs of healthy LTV:CAC (mainly in Starter/Growth), with CAC still calibrating.
- **Developer ecosystem**: open-source libraries published with initial developer community (500+ GitHub stars, 100+ sandbox users).

## Glossary (acronyms and terms)

- **API**: Application Programming Interface; interface for integration between systems.
- **BD**: Business Development; business/partnership development.
- **AE**: Account Executive; account executive (conducts sale and contract closing).
- **DX**: Developer Experience; quality of the developer's interaction with tools, documentation, and APIs.
- **MSA**: Master Service Agreement; master service agreement.
- **DPA**: Data Processing Addendum; data processing addendum/term (data protection and obligations between parties).
- **MRR**: Monthly Recurring Revenue; monthly recurring revenue.
- **ARR**: Annual Recurring Revenue; annual recurring revenue.
- **FinTech**: financial technology company.
- **MVP**: Minimum Viable Product; minimum product version to validate hypotheses with customers.
- **Pre-seed/Seed/Series A**: typical funding stages (from initial validation to expansion).
- **Runway**: time the company can operate with available cash, given burn rate.
- **SRE**: Site Reliability Engineering; discipline of system reliability/operability.
